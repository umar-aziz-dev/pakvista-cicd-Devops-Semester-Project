About Lahore

Lahore, the provincial capital of Punjab, is widely regarded as the cultural, intellectual, and artistic heart of Pakistan. Known as the "City of Gardens" for its lush public parks and the verdant Shalimar Gardens, it is also celebrated as a living museum of Mughal and Sikh architectural genius. The walled city, bazaars overflowing with spices and textiles, and an unmatched food street scene make Lahore one of South Asia's most captivating urban destinations.

From the roaring cannon of the Badshahi Mosque's Azaan echoing across the skyline to the evening aarti at the Samadhi of Ranjit Singh, Lahore is a city where every alley holds a story centuries old. For travellers, artists, historians, and food enthusiasts alike, Lahore offers an experience that is both humbling and exhilarating.

Top Attractions

Badshahi Mosque

Commissioned by the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb in 1671, the Badshahi Mosque is one of the largest mosques in the world and an unrivalled symbol of Mughal architectural perfection. Its vast open courtyard can accommodate more than 100,000 worshippers. Constructed entirely from red sandstone and white marble, the mosque's three majestic domes and four towering minarets rise dramatically against the Lahore skyline.

Visitors are welcomed to explore the courtyard and the museum within the mosque premises, which houses sacred relics. The view from the neighbouring Hazuri Bagh garden at sunset, with the mosque's domes bathed in golden light, is an unforgettable sight. Modest dress is required for entry.

Visitor Info

Open daily from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM. Entry is free. Modest attire is mandatory – headscarves and shalwar kameez are available for loan at the entrance.

Lahore Fort (Shahi Qila)

Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1981, Lahore Fort is a monumental complex spanning 20 hectares in the heart of the walled city. The fort's origins trace back to the 11th century, but its present grandeur is owed to successive Mughal emperors from Akbar to Aurangzeb, each adding palaces, mosques, gardens, and audience halls that reflect their individual tastes.

Among the fort's most celebrated structures are the Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors), whose walls and ceilings are adorned with thousands of tiny convex mirrors that create a dazzling mosaic effect, the Diwan-e-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), the Naulakha Pavilion with its intricate pietra dura inlay work, and the Alamgiri Gate – a commanding baroque-style entrance built by Aurangzeb facing the Badshahi Mosque.

Visitor Info

Open Tuesday to Sunday, 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM (closed Mondays). Entry fee applies. Guided tours are available in Urdu and English at the main gate.

Shalimar Gardens

Designed under the patronage of Emperor Shah Jahan and completed in 1641 CE, the Shalimar Gardens are a Mughal masterpiece of landscape architecture spread over 16 hectares in eastern Lahore. Also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the gardens are laid out in a formal Persian charbagh style – divided into three descending terraces symbolising paradise, representing the levels of heaven, earth, and the underworld.

Each terrace is connected by a central water channel fed by the historic Hansi Canal. At the height of the Mughal era, the gardens contained 410 marble-clad fountains, 5 large cascade waterfalls, and numerous pavilions and kiosks. Ornamental fruit trees including mangoes, apricots, mulberries, and pomegranates shade the walkways, creating a cool, fragrant retreat from the city's heat. Restoration work has revived much of the original water system, and the fountains are operational during special cultural events.

Visitor Info

Open daily, 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM. A nominal entry fee is charged. Best visited in the morning to enjoy the gardens before the midday heat.
